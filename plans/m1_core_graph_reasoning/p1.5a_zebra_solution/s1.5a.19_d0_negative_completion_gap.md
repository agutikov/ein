## BUG

### Observed end-state

```
$ ./bench_solve_pypy.sh --hyp-stat -v ./examples/zebra2.ein \
    --progress-every 100 --dump-config --max-depth 4 \
    --dump-states ./dump/zebra2
… verdict: Ambiguity  (solutions=0, dead=472, open=77, tree_nodes=568, 49.9s)
```

NL solves with **four** refuted d=1 branches and saturation
between them ([`examples/README.md`](../../../examples/README.md)).
Engine explores 568 nodes at depth ≤ 4 and produces **zero**
solution leaves.

### Where the engine actually closes the NL Step-1 inferences

Reading [`dump/zebra2/resats/`](../../../dump/zebra2/resats/) — the
**root**'s re-saturation events, fired by the S1.5.7b consume-loop
when one or more children die unconditionally — reveals that
**NL's Step-1 closure does land**, just not at d=0:

| event | dead children (back-prop sources) | new derivations on root |
|---|---|---|
| `resats/001` (cycle 1) | 11 d=1 branches (b1–b11) die unconditionally; each calls `back_propagate(root_kb, h_i, unsat_core)` → 11 `(not h_i)` writes | `range-elimination color-loc` ⟹ `(color-loc Yellow House-1)`; `co-located` ⟹ `(smoke-loc Kools House-1)`; `adjacent-via-fwd next-to` ⟹ `(pet-loc Horse House-2)`; 5 more derived negatives |
| `resats/002` (cycle 2) | 13 more d=1 branches (b12–b24) die; 13 `(not h_i)` writes | `domain-elimination drink-loc` ⟹ `(drink-loc Water House-1)` |

Every fact NL derives at d=0 in Step 1 **is in the engine's kb**
by the end of cycle 2 — but at a cost of 24 fully-saturated dead
d=1 branches (firings per branch: 233 for b6, 263 for b1, …).

The mechanism is the **S1.5a.14 transitive back-prop**: an
unconditional death on a d=1 branch with unsat-core grounded in
given facts bubbles `(not h)` to root via
[`back_propagate`](../../../ein.py/src/ein_bot/inference/back_prop.py);
when the consume-loop sweep closes, root re-saturates with the
new negatives, and `domain-elimination` / `range-elimination` /
`co-located` / `adjacent-via-fwd` fire on the now-richer kb.
**Concrete example:** branch `b6_color-loc_red_house-1` has
`unsat_core = {Norwegian@H_1, Red@H_1}` and 233 firings —
exactly the NL two-step chain *"Norwegian@H_1 ⟹ Englishman ≠ H_1
⟹ Red ≠ H_1"*, but expressed as fork + saturate + contradiction
detect + back-prop rather than a d=0 rule firing.

So the **functional case (gap 1)** and the **co-located chain
(gap 3)** that the previous draft of this doc labelled "missing"
are in fact **closed via refutation** — slower, but sound. The
**spatial endpoint (gap 2)** is also closed by `b2 (Green@H_1)`
and `b4 (Ivory@H_5)` dying directly.

### The two open questions

#### Q1 — d=0 inference incompleteness (perf, not correctness)

The engine's `01_root_saturated.ein` carries only 3 positive
`*-loc` facts and 3 negative ones; everything else is discovered
via refutation. NL closes the same fact-set in **one** d=0
saturation pass. The gap is **branch-budget-per-discovered-
negative**: 24 d=1 branches each saturating ~250 times to learn
24 negatives that direct d=0 rules could derive in one pass.

This is a perf gap, not a correctness gap. The fix is to add
inference rules that derive these negatives directly at d=0 —
see "Design space" in T1.5a.19.4.

#### Q2 — Post-Step-1-closure downstream stall

After cycle 2 the root kb has all 4 NL Step-1 positives + a
batch of feeding negatives, yet the engine continues exploring
544 more nodes (b25 onward) without reaching a solution. The
enhanced dumper (T1.5a.19.3) surfaced three concrete sub-issues
through the `00_timeline.jsonl` ordering + the resat attribution
split:

##### Q2a — Stale alive-set under `enable_alive_inherit=True` **(confirmed)**

`kb.alive` is computed once at root saturation
([`solver.py:1442`](../../../ein.py/src/ein_bot/inference/solver.py))
and inherited by forks. Resats add facts but `kb.alive` is **not**
recomputed. Timeline evidence — root cycle 1 fires at
``seq=25, ts_ms=2487`` and writes ``(color-loc Yellow House-1)``;
then `b15 (Yellow@H_3)` is alloc'd at ``seq=32, ts_ms=3417`` and
`b20 (Yellow@H_4)` at ``seq=42`` — both **after** cycle 1's
write, both **immediately falsifiable** by functional color-loc
against Yellow@H_1, both still forked and saturated (1050+
firings each). The frozen alive set carries the stale candidates
across the resat. **Fix candidate:** recompute (or prune)
``kb.alive`` against the new ``_negated_facts`` index at the end
of each `node_resaturated` on the same kb.

##### Q2b — 0-derivation resats **(confirmed)**

`b25 cycle 1` (seq=86): **10 back-prop writes, 0 derivations.**
The 10 negatives written are
``¬{Red@H_3, Red@H_4, Englishman@H_3, Englishman@H_4,
Tea@H_5, Japanese@H_5, Spaniard@H_5, Ukrainian@H_5,
Dog@H_5, Parliament@H_5}`` — surface-rich, semantically
dense — yet the post-write saturation pass produces no new
fact. Why? `range-elimination` needs **every** alternative
excluded for a given fact-slot, and the post-write KB still
has gaps: e.g. ``¬Red@H_2`` is **not** known to root (no
functional-derived negative ever lands), so even with
``¬Red@H_1, ¬Red@H_3, ¬Red@H_4``, range-elimination can't
fire on Red. The back-prop chain only delivers the
**hypothesised** negatives (one per dead candidate), not the
**implicit** negatives that the functional / injective
constraints make true. **This is the same gap that drives Q1**
— without a functional-negative rule, the cascade never
ignites. Designing the fix in T1.5a.19.4 simultaneously
unblocks Q1 and Q2b.

##### Q2c — Missed contradiction post-resat **(confirmed — likely a separate engine bug)**

`b432 cycle 2` (seq=939, ``smoke-loc Chesterfields House-3``
under ``color-loc Green House-4``) **derives ``(false)``** via
``functional drink-loc`` on the contradictory pair
``Coffee@H_4`` + ``Coffee@H_5`` produced earlier in the same
saturation cascade. Yet b432's `node_dump` at seq=966 reports
``verdict=open, unsat=0``; and the engine continues alloc-ing
d=3 children of b432 (b468–b495+) for **~20 seconds** after the
``(false)`` derivation. Re-saturation produces a contradiction
that the engine doesn't act on — either ContradictionDetector
isn't re-run after `_consume`'s resat pass, or the verdict-write
happens in a code path that doesn't observe the post-resat kb.
**Fix candidate:** re-run ``ContradictionDetector(kb).detect()``
inside ``_consume`` after the final resat cycle on a node; on
any hit, mark the node dead + back-prop ``(not own_hypothesis)``
+ skip child exploration. **Likely a new sub-stage S1.5a.19a
(or absorbed into S1.5a.15 P2's promoted-dead path).**

##### Scope note — hypothesis order is out

Hypothesis ordering ([S1.5a.7](s1.5a.7_hypgen_scoring_branch_info.md))
affects *time-to-first-solution*, not the closure under
``max_depth``. The set of facts derivable at depth ``d`` is
fixed by the root kb + rule set + the *set* of length-≤``d``
path conditions; permuting the order of those paths produces
the same closure. So S1.5a.19's correctness/completeness axis
is independent of order — order is a perf knob, tracked under
[S1.5a.7](s1.5a.7_hypgen_scoring_branch_info.md), and explicitly
out of scope here.

### Dumper gaps (your observation 2026-05-25)

Reading the resat artefacts surfaced **two limits of the
[S1.5a.11](s1.5a.11_state_dump.md) dump format** that hid this
finding from the first analysis pass:

1. **No global timeline.** `resats/001.json` says cycle 1 fired
   on node 0 with `dead_children = [b1, …, b11]`, but the reader
   has to cross-reference against the branch-allocation order
   under `branches/` to learn that cycle 1 ran *between
   exploring b11 and b12*. There is no top-level `00_timeline.txt`
   or analogous record of "step k: explored b_n; step k+1: resat
   cycle c on node m". For tree-with-resats traces, that
   ordering is the whole story.

2. **No per-fact bubble-source attribution.** `negatives_added`
   in a `node_resaturated` event is one flat list of 19 entries
   (cycle 1) or 14 entries (cycle 2). The first
   `len(dead_children)` entries happen to be the
   `back_propagate` writes positionally aligned with
   `dead_children` (verified by inspection); the remainder are
   re-saturation derivations. The JSON does not tag the boundary
   or label per-entry whether it came from back-prop or from
   the new saturator pass, nor (for re-sat derivations) the
   `:rule` provenance the underlying Fact carries — that
   provenance IS in the corresponding `<cycle>.ein` (e.g.,
   `(color-loc Yellow House-1 :rule "range-elimination" :layer
   reasoning)`) but the JSON loses it. Bridging back to a
   *specific* dead child for the deep case ("b432 died because
   b432's descendant b{deeper} bubbled (not h_deeper) into b432")
   requires walking the unsat-core in the deeper branch's
   `verdict.json` by hand.

T1.5a.19.3 (below) is the dumper enhancement that addresses both.

### T1.5a.19.2 — Downstream-stall measurement (closed 2026-05-26)

**Status:** Q2a empirically subsumed; Q2b closed by the
T1.5a.19.4 ein-rule landing; Q2c not observed at zebra2 d=1 —
remains a theoretical hardening opportunity if a future puzzle
exhibits sweep-produced contradictions.

**Q2a (stale alive-set on resat) — SUBSUMED 2026-05-26.** The
per-sibling apriori re-check shipped in
[`solver.py:1075-1122`](../../../ein.py/src/ein_bot/inference/solver.py)
consults `kb._negated_facts` directly *before* `try_branch`, so
any sibling whose negation lands during the sweep is dropped
in O(1) — no fork, no saturate. Combined with the mid-sweep
`Saturator(kb).saturate(...)` after every `back_propagate`, the
new d=0 negative-completion rules (T1.5a.19.4) transitively
expand the parent kb's `_negated_facts` set as siblings die,
which the next sibling's pre-fork check then consumes. The
inherited `kb.alive` set is effectively pruned per-sibling at
the parent level without an explicit `_prune_alive` call.

*Evidence.* `bench_solve_pypy.sh examples/zebra2.ein --max-depth 1`
(post-`455bfd6`, fresh dump 2026-05-26) reports 28 of 31 dead
leaves as `apriori_dead_in_sweep` — 90 % of the sibling-death
work skipped `try_branch` entirely. Pre-S1.5a.19, all 31 would
have been forked. The original Q2a symptom (b15, b20 being
forked for Yellow@H3/H4 after cycle-1 derived Yellow@H1) is
gone: in the current dump, Yellow@H{3,4,5} candidates either
don't appear in the root alive-set (filtered at hypgen) or are
caught by the apriori re-check.

**Q2b (cascade ignition for back-propped negatives) — CLOSED.**
The six new ein rules in `examples/zebra2.ein` derive the
"implicit" negatives (functional-negative, injective-negative,
co-located-negative, adjacent-via-endpoint-{fwd,bwd},
adjacent-via-{fwd,bwd}-negative) that domain/range-elimination
needed but never saw pre-S1.5a.19. Verified end-to-end —
zebra2 solves at `--max-depth 1` with 32 nodes / 1 solution.

**Q2c (missed contradiction post-resat) — NOT OBSERVED at d=1;
code-path concern remains.** The fresh dump has a single root
`node_resaturated` event (cycle 1: 3 dead children → 3
back-prop writes → 53 resat derivations → 2138 firings) and
then drains to Solution. No node ever runs a mid-sweep
saturator that produces (`false`) or a `(X, (not X))` pair —
so the bug doesn't bite at the current scope.

*Code-path note.* `_consume`'s sweep does NOT call
`ContradictionDetector(kb).detect()` after its mid-sweep
`Saturator(kb).saturate(...)`. If a future puzzle's d=0
rule library combined with back-propped negatives produced a
contradictory parent kb mid-sweep, the engine would miss it
until the next descent's `_explore_inner` ran the detector
([`solver.py:711`](../../../ein.py/src/ein_bot/inference/solver.py))
— one step late, not lost. Hardening:

- **Option (a):** Run `ContradictionDetector(kb).detect()` after
  every `mid_sweep_firings.extend(...)`. If non-empty, mark
  the sweep complete + back-prop `(not own_hypothesis)` to the
  grandparent. ~5 LOC; cost dominated by the detect scan.
- **Option (b):** Defer — Q2c didn't bite zebra2 d=1; if a
  deeper puzzle reproduces, promote (a) to a small follow-up
  sub-stage.

Defaulting to (b); leaving the hardening as a parked option in
this stage. The Q2c BUG description (b432 / `functional drink-loc`
on Coffee@H4 vs Coffee@H5) was driven by pre-S1.5a.19's deep
search exhaustion; the depth-1 solve makes that scenario
unreachable.

### T1.5a.19.4 — Catalog missing d=0 inferences + design space

For each NL row in `examples/README.md` at `d = 0`, classify the
inference as:

- **direct-saturation** — engine derives it during root saturation
  (the 3 facts in `01_root_saturated.ein`).
- **refutation-derived** — engine derives it via back-prop +
  resat (the cycle-1/2 set). Sub-classify by the underlying
  inference shape (functional-negative, injective-negative,
  spatial-endpoint, co-located-chain). Counts feed the design
  space below.
- **lookahead-pruned-late** — engine drops the negation of this
  candidate at hypgen time via lookahead, but does not
  re-saturate, so domain-elim doesn't see it at d=0.

Design space — three plausible fixes for moving refutation-derived
inferences to direct-saturation:

| approach | locus | shape |
|---|---|---|
| **A. ein rules** | `examples/zebra2.ein` | add ein rules like `(rule functional-negative (?R) :match (and (functional ?R) (?R ?a ?b) (relation ?R ?A ?B) (is-a ?b_other ?B) (neq ?b_other ?b)) :assert (not (?R ?a ?b_other)) :priority 240)` — no engine change; `domain-elimination`'s `forall` premise consumes the explicit `(not …)` facts the rule emits. |
| **B. engine semantics** | `ein.py/src/ein_bot/inference/saturator.py` | extend `functional`/`injective` to fire both `(false)` on violation AND `(not …)` on every alternative when the positive is single. Keeps ein rule-set lean; centralises the negative-derivation logic. |
| **C. hybrid: lookahead → saturation feedback** | `ein.py/src/ein_bot/inference/{lookahead,saturator}.py` + `solver.py` | re-enter saturation after hypgen has lookahead-killed candidates and back-propagated negatives, so domain-elim sees the new `(not …)` facts at d=0. Cheapest engine touch; covers the *one-step* subset, leaves two-step chains (Norwegian → ¬Englishman → ¬Red) and spatial-endpoint to A or B. |

Open questions:

- **Cost.** A inflates the firing count quadratically per
  bijection (5 relations × ~25 negatives per known fact). B
  has the same algorithmic shape but fires inside the matcher
  loop (cheaper per write, harder to attribute in traces). C
  is bounded by the lookahead's existing budget.
- **Trace fidelity (idea-08 / S1.5a.12 target).** A matches the
  NL's `(functional color-loc) → negative-derivation`
  annotation verbatim; B needs a synthetic provenance; C
  attributes to `<lookahead-dies-immediately>`.
- **Soundness inside `try_branch`.** Adding `(not (R a b'))`
  from a hypothesised `(R a b)` is fine — the death back-props
  it away on unconditional refutation. Provenance must carry
  the hypothesis so the back-prop attribution stays correct.
- **Spatial endpoint.** A's coverage is natural — add
  `(adjacent-via-endpoint-{fwd,bwd})` rules with
  `(forall ?h_other (is-a ?h_other House) (not (?S ?h_excluded ?h_other)))`
  in the body. B has nowhere natural for it (engine has no
  spatial primitives).

Output: `s1.5a.19_d0_catalog.md` (per-row table) + `s1.5a.19_design.md`
(picked approach + spec).
