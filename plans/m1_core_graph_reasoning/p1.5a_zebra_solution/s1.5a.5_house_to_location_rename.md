## What changed

### Relation names + arg order

Five attribute relations renamed and arg-swapped:

| before                            | after                             |
|-----------------------------------|-----------------------------------|
| `(relation house-color  House Color)`        | `(relation color-loc  Color House)`        |
| `(relation house-nation House Nationality)`  | `(relation nation-loc Nationality House)`  |
| `(relation house-drink  House Drink)`        | `(relation drink-loc  Drink House)`        |
| `(relation house-smoke  House Cigarette)`    | `(relation smoke-loc  Cigarette House)`    |
| `(relation house-pet    House Pet)`          | `(relation pet-loc    Pet House)`          |

The signature swap (Attribute first, House second) aligns the
shape with the "location of X" reading: `(color-loc Red H1)`
reads as "the location of Red is H1".

### Two adjacent cross-attribute primitives

| before                | after          | extra change                                                                 |
|-----------------------|----------------|------------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| `linked`              | `co-located`   | rule body's `(?R1 ?h ?V1)` → `(?R1 ?V1 ?h)` (attribute slot 0).               |
| `linked-symmetric`    | `co-located-symmetric` | meta-rule that derives the reverse activator; renamed in lockstep.  |
| `cross-attr-spatial`         | `adjacent-via`         | `?S` (the spatial relation) moves to the first activator arg.       |
| `cross-attr-spatial-fwd/bwd` | `adjacent-via-fwd/bwd` | rule body swaps `(?R1 ?h1 ?V1)` → `(?R1 ?V1 ?h1)` and assert mirror. |
| `derive-cross-attr-spatial-*` | `derive-adjacent-via-*` | rename only; activator-args reorder for `?S` first.                |
| `derive-disjunctive-prune-*`  | (kept name)            | activator-args mirror `adjacent-via-*`'s reorder.                  |

The `disjunctive-prune` rule body also flipped its
`(is-a ?h_other ?A)` premise to `(is-a ?h_other ?B)` because the
relation-signature destructuring `(relation ?R2 ?A ?B)` now binds
`?B` to House (post-swap).

### Activator + fact text

`(facts)` rewrites:

```lisp
;; before
(linked house-nation Englishman house-color Red :source "(2)")
(cross-attr-spatial house-color Ivory house-color Green right-of :source "(6)")
(house-nation House-1 Norwegian :source "(10)")

;; after
(co-located nation-loc Englishman color-loc Red :source "(2)")
(adjacent-via right-of color-loc Ivory color-loc Green :source "(6)")
(nation-loc Norwegian House-1 :source "(10)")
```

`(query)` rewrite:

```lisp
;; before
:goal (and (house-drink ?h_water Water) (house-pet ?h_zebra Zebra))
:hrules (guess (house-color House Color) (house-nation House Nationality) …)

;; after
:goal (and (drink-loc Water ?h_water) (pet-loc Zebra ?h_zebra))
:hrules (guess (color-loc Color House) (nation-loc Nationality House) …)
```

The query goal's open-variable position swapped too — `?h_water`
is now in slot 1 because the relation is `(drink-loc Drink House)`.

## What didn't change

- **Semantics**: every rule keeps its logical content. The arg-
  swap shows up in `(R ?V ?h)` instead of `(R ?h ?V)` patterns;
  the matcher binds positionally so the algebra is identical.
- **Saturation behaviour**: 223 total firings (same as pre-rename),
  181 productive, `naf_dropped == 0`.
- **The hrule body**: `(hrule guess (?R ?T1 ?T2) :match (and (is-a
  ?a ?T1) (is-a ?b ?T2)) :assert (?R ?a ?b))` — variable names
  renamed from `?h ?v` → `?a ?b` for clarity (they were misleading
  post-swap), but the logic is unchanged.
- **Spatial relations**: `right-of` and `next-to` stay
  `(House, House)` — no arg-swap.
- **`is-a`**: untouched; its arg order is conventional
  (`(is-a House-1 House)` reads "House-1 is-a House").

## Open questions

- **Q-S1.5a.5.A — Argument order swap.** ~~Should the rename also
  swap arg order…~~ Resolved 2026-05-24: yes, swapped. The
  semantic equivalence under bijection makes either order
  workable; the "location of X" reading is more natural with
  attribute in slot 0.
- **Q-S1.5a.5.B — Generalisation to non-zebra puzzles.** `*-loc`
  is **zebra-specific** stylistic naming. Other puzzles with no
  positional carrier should keep the attribute-functional naming
  (`house-color`-style or whatever fits). Document this as a
  positional-puzzle convention, not a kernel rule.
- **Q-S1.5a.5.C — Should `adjacent-via` be split into
  `adjacent-fwd` / `adjacent-bwd` activators rather than a single
  activator that expands?** No — the single-activator + derive-fwd
  + derive-bwd pattern is one declaration per puzzle condition,
  which is what authors want.
