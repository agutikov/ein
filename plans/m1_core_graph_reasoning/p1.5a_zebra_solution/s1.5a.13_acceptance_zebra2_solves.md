#### Comparison vs pre-S1.5a.19 baseline

The pre-S1.5a.19 baseline was recorded at
[STATUS.md § Smoke](STATUS.md) on PyPy3 (depth 4 — depth 1 was
insufficient to drive the engine to any verdict because the
d=0 negative-completion gap blocked the disjunctive split):

| metric                | pre-S1.5a.19 (depth 4)  | post-S1.5a.19 (depth 1) | delta              |
|-----------------------|-------------------------|-------------------------|--------------------|
| verdict               | **Ambiguity**           | **Solution**            | qualitative change |
| tree nodes            | 568                     | 32                      | **−94 %** (−17.8×) |
| dead leaves           | 472                     | 31                      | −93 %              |
| open leaves           | 77                      | 0                       | terminal           |
| solution leaves       | 0                       | 1                       | terminal           |
| solve wall (PyPy)     | 49.9 s                  | 8.4 s                   | **−83 %**          |

The dominant win is the verdict flip (Ambiguity → Solution at
shallower depth); the 6× wall-time improvement is the
secondary effect of the smaller tree. The six new d=0 rules
([s1.5a.19](s1.5a.19_d0_negative_completion_gap.md)) +
per-sibling apriori re-check + mid-sweep saturator pass do the
work of what previously required hypothesis branching to
discover.

- **PyPy3**: **8.4 s** — well inside budget (14 % of ceiling).
- **CPython 3.14**: **50.9 s** — inside budget (85 % of
  ceiling). Marginal; deeper puzzles or rule-library growth
  could push CPython past 60 s. PyPy is the recommended
