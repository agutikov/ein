## Context

Today's `AbsentGuard` (post-S1.5.8c-3) compiles its sub-plan
once at rule-compile time and evaluates the sub-plan against
the KB state at **first-enqueue time**. The firing then commits
regardless of later KB state — by the time the firing is
dequeued, the AbsentGuard's "I saw no fact matching X" snapshot
may be stale.

Concretely (from the parent README):

- `cross-attr-spatial-fwd` for condition (12) `(Kools next-to
  Horse)` fires deterministically for non-corner Kools houses,
  inventing a unique-neighbour conclusion when the puzzle's
  semantics says the case is disjunctive.
- `disjunctive-prune` (intended to derive negatives for
  non-adjacent houses) has the same exposure — same NAF shape.

The current workaround in `examples/zebra2.ein` is to declare
next-to facts explicitly in `(facts)` (8 facts replacing 4
`right-of` + `symmetric`/`includes` derivation). Dodges the
race for *this* relation; doesn't fix the underlying semantics.

## Approach options

Reproduced + sharpened from the parent README:

### (1) Engine: re-evaluate NAF at fire time

When a queued firing dequeues, re-run its match (or just its
NAF sub-plans) against the current KB. If NAF now fails, drop
the firing. ~15-30 LOC in `inference/saturator.py`'s `_apply`.

- **Pro:** most principled. Fixes every NAF-on-derived-fact
  case at once, not just the spatial ones in zebra2.
- **Con:** changes the engine's enqueue ⇒ commit invariant.
  Need to think about whether dropped firings interact with the
  fixpoint termination check.

This is the long-term answer. T1.5a.1.1 explores it.

### (2) Per-rule "wait for closure" gating

Defer rules whose NAF depends on derived facts until those
facts are saturated.

- **Needs** a "phase marker" mechanism the engine doesn't have
  today.
- **Pro:** preserves the enqueue ⇒ commit invariant.
- **Con:** requires a static analysis of which NAFs depend on
  which derived facts; engineering surface area larger than (1).

T1.5a.1.2 sketches the static-analysis dependency.

### (3) Ein-side: pre-declare derived facts

Sidestep the race by manually enumerating the derived facts in
`(facts)` or `(ontology)` (already done for next-to in zebra2).

- **Pro:** trivial; no engine change.
- **Con:** per-puzzle, doesn't generalise.

T1.5a.1.3 documents the workaround pattern for the M1-acceptance
shortcut. (1) is the durable answer; (3) keeps the lights on
while (1) is being designed.

## Tasks

### Task T1.5a.1.1 — Re-evaluate NAF at fire time

- **a.** Identify the dequeue path in `saturator.py` `_apply`;
  insert a re-evaluation step that re-runs the firing's
  `AbsentGuard` sub-plans against current KB.
- **b.** If re-eval fails, drop the firing; record metric
  `naf_re_eval_dropped` for visibility.
- **c.** Re-run zebra2 with derived next-to (the
  `right-of`+`symmetric`+`includes` chain rather than 8 explicit
  next-to facts); verify that `cross-attr-spatial-fwd` no longer
  fires the spurious unique-neighbour conclusions.
- **d.** Termination invariant: confirm a dropped firing doesn't
  prevent saturation from reaching fixpoint (re-enqueue if any
  watched fact changes? Document the invariant either way.).
- **e.** Tests: at least 3 saturation tests that exercise NAF
  over derived facts and pass under fire-time re-eval, fail
  under enqueue-time eval. Plus a regression: zebra2 derived
  next-to solves uniquely.

### Task T1.5a.1.2 — Static NAF dependency map (option 2)

Independent of T1.5a.1.1's fix; surfaces what's at risk.

- **a.** For each rule with `AbsentGuard` premises, compute the
  set of relation names the NAF references.
- **b.** Cross-reference: which of those relations are
  **derived** (have at least one rule that asserts them) vs
  **declared-only** (only appear in `(facts)` and
  `(ontology)`)?
- **c.** Emit a warning when a derived-NAF rule is loaded,
  pointing at the specific relations the rule's correctness
  depends on.
- **d.** Smoke test: zebra2 emits the warning for
  `cross-attr-spatial-fwd` and `disjunctive-prune` before
  T1.5a.1.1 lands; emits none after, if those NAFs are
  re-evaluated soundly.

### Task T1.5a.1.3 — Ein-side workaround pattern docs

- **b.** Note the limitation: works per-puzzle, doesn't

## Open questions

- **Q-S1.5a.1.A — Termination invariant under fire-time NAF
  re-eval.** Does dropping a firing late in the cycle still
  guarantee saturation termination? Worked example: rule A's
  NAF refers to relation R; rule B asserts R. A enqueues, then
  B fires asserting R, then A's NAF would fail at fire time. A
  is dropped. If A's conclusion was needed by C, does C still
  fire correctly without A? (Probably yes — A was a *false*
  derivation.) Document the invariant.
- **Q-S1.5a.1.B — Per-NAF caching.** Re-evaluating every
  firing's NAF at fire time is O(NAFs × firings). Worth caching
  the NAF result per `(rule, binding)` and invalidating on
  watched-fact arrival? Composes with [P1.9 E8 watched-fact
  rule applicability](../p1.9_hypothesis_loop_followups/README.md).
