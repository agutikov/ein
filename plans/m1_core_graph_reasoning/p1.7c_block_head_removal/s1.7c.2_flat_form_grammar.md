## Implementation notes (2026-06-02)

- **Wrappers kept, not removed (deprecation window).** `?form` now lists the
  four wrappers *and* the flat alternatives (`relation_decl | rule_decl |
  hrule_decl | query_form | config_form | trace_form | fact_form`). Removing
  the wrapper productions here would make every still-wrapped `.ein` /
  fixture fail to parse (or worse, mis-lower a `(rule …)` to a `generic_list`
  with the wrong `@params` shape) before [S1.7c.4](s1.7c.4_migrate_and_drop_shim.md)
  migrates them. So the productions + their transformer methods stay until
  S1.7c.4; `ast.py` is unchanged this stage (`start()` already returns a flat
  tuple). Wrappers listed first so Earley resolves `(ontology …)` to
  `ontology_form`, not `generic_fact(ontology)`. Verified: all 53 wrapped
  examples still parse + load identically.
- **Reserved-head collision (Q3) — declarators shape-pinned at the lexer.**
  The declarator heads `rule|hrule|query|config|trace` are added to the
  `SYMBOL` negative-lookahead (joining `not|and|or|neq`). This keeps the
  *parse-time* shape guards a flat top level would otherwise lose: a malformed
  `(rule x)` / `(query)` / `(trace (foo …))` can no longer fall through to
  `generic_fact` (their heads aren't `SYMBOL`), so they stay parse errors —
  exactly as before. `relation` is the **one** declarator left in `SYMBOL`
  (rules match `(relation ?R ?A ?B)` patterns); its malformed form stays a
  *load*-time rejection (pre-existing `test_relation_wrapped_form_rejected_at_load`).
  The former wrappers `ontology|facts|reasoning|rules` are deliberately left
  in `SYMBOL` — post-migration they become ordinary fact heads. Caveat
  (documented in [S1.7c.5](s1.7c.5_docs_grammar_reserved_names.md)): like the
  existing primitives, an identifier may not *equal* a reserved word nor begin
  with `<word>-` (e.g. `rule-x`); nothing in the corpus does.
- **Tests updated to the flat contract.** 8 grammar tests encoded the old
  "top level = wrappers only" strictness; rewritten to the flat semantics:
  `(unknown-head …)` / `(step …)` / `(branch-open …)` / `(ontology :foo bar)`
  now parse as **facts**; `(and|or|neq …)` rejection moved to top level; the
  CLI `ir lint` failure fixture switched from `(unknown-head a b)` (now valid)
  to `(and a b)`. The 4 reserved-shape guards above pass **unchanged**.

## Goal

Turn the top level of [`grammar.lark`](../../../ein.py/src/ein/ir/grammar.lark)
from "exactly 7 reserved block heads" into a **flat sequence of forms**, each
classified by its head against the closed declarator set.

Today: `start: form*` where `?form` is one of `ontology_form` / `facts_form` /
`reasoning_form` / `rules_form` / `query_form` / `trace_form` / `config_form`,
and the block productions wrap their children.

## Tasks

- **Top level → flat.** Replace the seven block productions with a top-level
  alternation over the *child* productions:
  `?form: relation_decl | rule_decl | hrule_decl | query_form | config_form |
  trace_form | fact_form` (+ `macro_decl` once [P1.8 S1.5.9](../p1.8_ein_lang_modules/s1.5.9_ein_lang_macros.md)
  lands). `start: form*` stays — `(program …)` root rejected (Q4: a bare
  sequence; nothing needs a root node).
- **Classification = head against the reserved declarator set.** The
  `SYMBOL` lexer already excludes the kernel meta-primitives (`not`/`and`/
  `or`/`neq`); the *declarator* heads (`relation`/`rule`/`hrule`/`query`/
  `config`/`macro`) get their own literal-headed productions (higher lex
  priority than `SYMBOL`, like the existing `relation_decl`). **Everything
  else falls through to `generic_fact`** — exactly the author's intent
  ("detect facts by *not* being in the reserved set").
- **`trace` is a sibling, not a fact (Q3 note).** `(trace …)` is
  engine-*emitted* output, parsed by [`trace/ast.py`](../../../ein.py/src/ein/trace/ast.py),
  not by `from_ir.load` (which `pass`es on it, `from_ir.py:392`). Keep
  `trace_form` as a recognised top-level head so it never misclassifies as a
  fact; it is not part of the declarator-vs-fact dichotomy.
- **Pin the declarator set closed (Q3).** A fact whose head collides with a
  future declarator name would misclassify; make
  [`06_reserved_names.md`](../../../docs/kernel/ir/03-ein-lang/06_reserved_names.md)
  the parser's source of truth (the list lives in one place the grammar +
  loader both key on). Document the collision rule in the grammar header.
- **Transformer.** Update [`ir/ast.py`](../../../ein.py/src/ein/ir/ast.py)
  — the `ontology_form`/`facts_form`/`reasoning_form` handlers go away; the
  top level yields a flat `list[SForm]` the loader classifies.

## Acceptance

- A flat `.ein` (no wrappers) parses; the head-classification round-trips
  (`relation`→decl, `query`→query, bare head→fact).
- The kernel meta-primitive parse-time guards are **unchanged** (`(nto Foo)`
  still a parse error; `(relation R (T1 T2))` still rejected).
- Grammar + transformer tests green. (The *loader* wiring is S1.7c.3; this
  stage may keep the old loader behind the shim until then.)
