# S1.7c.21 — `snapshot` shallow-copies indexes instead of full rebuild

**Phase:** P1.7c, Track B.
**Estimate:** ~1–2 hr. Medium risk (warm path) — gate on snapshot tests +
bench.
**Depends on:** P1.7b baseline. Safer **after**
[S1.7c.22](s1.7c.22_typed_index_wrappers.md) (the copy contract becomes
explicit), but doable directly.
**Finding:** [F-KB-6](../p1.7b_review_and_refactor/findings.md#kb--data-model-kbpy).

## Goal

`snapshot()` (`store.py:649`) calls a **full `rebuild_indexes()`** per
recorded solution, even though it copies `facts` verbatim from a kb whose
indexes are already consistent. Pure wasted work on the per-solution path.

## Tasks

- Replace the `rebuild_indexes()` call in `snapshot` with a **shallow copy**
  of the (already in-place-maintained, post-S1.7b.4) index dicts — `dict(...)`
  the fact-derived indexes, share the post-load-immutable ones by reference
  (same invariant `fork` relies on).

## Acceptance

- Snapshot kb indexes **identical** to what a full rebuild would produce
  (assert in a test).
- Snapshot tests green; `bench_solve_monotonic_pypy.sh` ≤ baseline (snapshot
  is warm, not the hottest path, but the rebuild is gone).
- 3-variant gate byte-identical.
