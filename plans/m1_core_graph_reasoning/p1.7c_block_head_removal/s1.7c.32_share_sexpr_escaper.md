# S1.7c.32 — Share the S-expression string escaper (closes a round-trip bug)

**Phase:** P1.7c, Track B.
**Estimate:** ~1 hr. Low risk — but it **fixes a latent bug**, so it carries a
regression test (the one sanctioned behaviour change in this stage).
**Depends on:** P1.7b baseline.
**Finding:** [F-RTC-10](../p1.7b_review_and_refactor/findings.md#rtc--render--trace--cli--ir).

## Goal

The S-expression string-escaper (`\\ \" \n \t \r`) is duplicated:
`ir/dump.py:50` is the canonical full escaper, but `trace/ast.py:89`
(`step_to_ir`) escapes **only** `\\` and `\"`. A trace step whose `why` text
contains a newline therefore round-trips **inconsistently** between the two
dumpers — a real correctness gap, not just duplication. (Distinct from the
DOT `esc` of [S1.7c.25](s1.7c.25_shared_dot_emitter.md).)

## Tasks

- Share one `escape_string_literal(s)` (the full `\\ \" \n \t \r` set) in a
  neutral IR-string module; route both `ir/dump.py` and `trace/ast.py`
  through it.

## Acceptance

- **Regression test:** a trace step whose `why` contains `\n` (and `\t`)
  round-trips identically through both `step_to_ir` and the canonical dumper
  (fails before, passes after).
- Dump + trace round-trip tests green; full suite green.
