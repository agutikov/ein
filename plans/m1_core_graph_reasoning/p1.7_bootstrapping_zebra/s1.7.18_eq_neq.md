# S1.7.18 — hardcoded names `eq` / `neq` (built-in predicate registry)

**Phase:** P1.7 (bootstrapping) — per-name kernel-purity analysis.
**Parent:** [S1.7.7](s1.7.7_kernel_purity_analysis.md) (inventory).
**Class:** predicates — **registry-extensible** (already a data table).
**Verdict:** **stays kernel, but already least-hardcoded** — the names
live in a runtime-extensible dict, not in control flow.
**Status:** analysis.

## Call sites

- `predicates.py:76–79` — `_REGISTRY = {"eq": _eq, "neq": _neq}`; the
  Q33 (2026-05-20) M1 cap is exactly these two.
- `compile.py:346–347` — predicate dispatch: a premise whose head is
  `is_predicate(head)` compiles to a `Guard` opcode (evaluated against
  bindings, **not** looked up in the KB) rather than a `Scan`.
- `compile.py:349–351`, `kb/from_ir` — `neq` is also grammar-pinned
  (`neq_form`); the loader consults `is_predicate` to suppress phantom

## 1. Functionality connected to the name

Predicates are **computed guards**: a relation's truth is *data* (looked
up in facts); a predicate's truth is *computed* from the current bindings.
`(neq ?a ?c)` prunes a match branch when two slots resolve equal; `eq` the
dual. They are the kernel's structural logic guards — distinct from
relations precisely because they are not in the KB. The names route
through a **registry dict**, not `== "neq"` comparisons in the matcher: the
matcher asks `is_predicate(head)` generically and calls `get(head)`.

## 2. Consequences if removed

Drop `neq` and the Zebra rules that say "two *different* houses" (the bulk
of the uniqueness/elimination constraints) lose their distinctness guard —
the engine would match a house against itself, deriving false positives.
`neq` is **load-bearing for correctness** of any all-different reasoning.
`eq` is less used (most equality is structural unification) but symmetric
in role. Unlike the logical primitives, these are not the ⊥ calculus —
they are guard *functions* — so removal degrades expressiveness (no
computed guards) rather than collapsing the engine.

## 3. How it could be implemented in ein-lang rules (or elsewhere)

`neq`/`eq` **cannot** be ordinary rules — their truth is computed over
bindings, not stored, so no fact pattern expresses them (that is the
definition of a predicate vs a relation, per the module docstring). They
*could* be reframed as relations over a materialised equality table
(`(eq A A)` for every entity), but that is O(n²) facts and defeats the
point. So they stay *functions*. The design already keeps them as **data
in a registry** rather than hardcoded branches — the most ein-friendly
form short of full reflection.

## 4. How it could be implemented without sticking to the name

The matcher is **already name-agnostic**: it dispatches via
`predicates.is_predicate` / `get`, so adding a predicate is
`register("lt", _lt)` — no engine edit. The names `"eq"`/`"neq"` are
privileged only by being *pre-registered* and (for `neq`) grammar-pinned.
To fully de-privilege: drop the grammar `neq_form` special production
(let `neq` parse as a generic head, already mostly true) and let the
**puzzle/stdlib register** the predicates it needs — the
[F5 rules-as-data](../../followups/f5_rules_as_data.md) move applied to
guard functions. The registry seam (`register`) is exactly the extension
point; `eq`/`neq` are just the two M1 defaults.

## Decision (to record in [S1.7.7 T1.7.7.5](s1.7.7_kernel_purity_analysis.md))

Record `eq`/`neq` as **kernel built-ins behind a data registry** — the
least-hardcoded names in the inventory (no control-flow special-casing;
matcher already generic). The only residual hardcode is the grammar
`neq_form` production and the pre-population of the registry; both are
defensible M1 defaults. Future numeric/set predicates land via `register`,
no engine change ([followups, Q33 deferral](../../open_questions.md)).
