# S1.7.14 — hardcoded name `and` (premise conjunction)

**Phase:** P1.7 (bootstrapping) — per-name kernel-purity analysis.
**Parent:** [S1.7.7](s1.7.7_kernel_purity_analysis.md) (inventory).
**Class:** logical primitive — **structural sugar** over premise lists.
**Verdict:** **stays kernel** ([S1.7.2](s1.7.2_dynamic_vs_hardcoded.md)),
but nearly trivial: it is the implicit conjunction a `:match` already is.
**Status:** analysis.

## Call sites

- `compile.py:330–334` — `(and P1 P2 …)` in a `:match` flattens into
  **sibling premises in the same plan** (a join chain): conjunction is
  just "all premises must match".
- `compile.py:218,249` — `_desugar_open` / `_desugar_forall` *emit*
  `(and …)` as their expansion target.
- `nogoods.py:11` — docstring: a nogood clause is the `and` of its
  literals.

## 1. Functionality connected to the name

`and` is conjunction of match premises. But a rule `:match` body is
**already an implicit conjunction** — every top-level premise must match
for the rule to fire. So `(and P1 P2)` and writing `P1 P2` as two sibling
premises compile to the **identical** join plan. The explicit `and`
exists for two reasons only: (a) **nesting** — to put a conjunction
*inside* another form, e.g. the `(absent (and G …))` that `forall`
desugars to; (b) authoring clarity.

## 2. Consequences if removed

At top level: **none** — drop `(and P1 P2)`, write `P1 P2`. The only real
loss is **nested** conjunction: `_desugar_forall` /`_desugar_open` produce
`(absent (and …))`, and `(absent P)` takes a *single* sub-pattern; without
`and` those desugarings have no way to hand `absent` a two-clause body. So
`and`'s irreducible use is **as the body of another primitive**, not at
top level. Remove it and `forall`/`open` desugaring breaks unless `absent`
is taught to take a premise *list*.

## 3. How it could be implemented in ein-lang rules (or elsewhere)

`and` is the metalanguage's conjunction — not rule-derivable (a rule body
*is* an `and`). The reduction is the reverse: `(and …)` at top level is
**already** redundant with juxtaposition, so the kernel could drop the
top-level arm and keep `and` *only* as a sub-form constructor. There is no
"express `and` as a rule" — it is the glue rules are made of.

## 4. How it could be implemented without sticking to the name

Make conjunction **positional, not named**: a premise *list* is the
conjunction (it already is at top level); for nesting, let `absent` /
`forall` / `open` accept a *sequence* of sub-patterns and conjoin them
implicitly — then `(and …)` as an explicit head disappears entirely, the
list structure carrying the meaning. This is the cleanest de-naming in the
inventory after [`open`](s1.7.11_open.md): conjunction becomes a
*structural* property of premise sequences, named nowhere.

## Decision (to record in [S1.7.7 T1.7.7.5](s1.7.7_kernel_purity_analysis.md))

Record `and` as **kernel but eliminable-as-a-head**: top-level `and` is
already juxtaposition; its only load-bearing use is as a nested body
constructor for `absent`/`forall`/`open`, which a "premise-list" change to
those forms would absorb. Low-risk cleanup, not a soundness concern —
schedule with the `open`/`forall` desugaring rework.
