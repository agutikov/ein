## Re-surface (2026-06-15)

Per the user, E3's correct surface is a **query-named key `:no-hypothesis`**,
not the `(no-hypotheses R)` property fact this stub assumed. That makes it the
exclusion **dual of the already-shipped `:hypothesis-relations` whitelist**
(`HYPOTHESIS_RELATIONS`, `hypgen.py:53`; parsed `hypgen.py:342-360`; applied
`:249-251`): today you scope guessing by *listing* the relations to guess on;
`:no-hypothesis` lists the relations to *not* guess on. The inclusion form
already ships, so the capability E3 wants (per-relation "record but don't
speculate", with saturation rules on R still firing) is **largely available**;
the residual is wiring the exclusion key as a sibling of `HYPOTHESIS_RELATIONS`.

This also dissolves the stub's conflation worry: `:no-hypothesis` is a
**query/config key**, cleanly separate from `(closed R)` (a relation property
that *also* blocks rule-derivation). The property-fact framing in the tasks
below is superseded by the query-key surface.

## Context

`(no-hypotheses R)` is *distinct* from `(closed R)`: R stays
open-world (the saturator may still derive new R-facts), but
the **hypothesis generator never guesses** on R. Useful for
observational data where the user wants the engine to
*record* what it sees but never *speculate* about gaps.
