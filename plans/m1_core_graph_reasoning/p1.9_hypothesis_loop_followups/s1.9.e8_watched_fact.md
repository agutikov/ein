## Superseded by P1.8a (2026-06-15)

The cost E8 set out to kill — *"avoid the re-iterate-every-applicable-rule-on-
every-saturation-pass cost"* — was eliminated by **P1.8a's incremental
saturation** (`S1.8.B2v` D2+D5, commits `ac7a694`/`3c0181b`) plus the **matcher
participation index** (`S1.8.B-idx`, `8222751`), all landing after the
2026-06-03 re-grounding:

- The saturator is now **delta-driven**: a derived fact wakes only plans whose
  positive premises reference its relation (`_pos_index`, `saturator.py:329-348`)
  and **seeds the match at the new fact** (`match.run_seeded`,
  `saturator.py:323-327`) rather than re-scanning — docstring: *"91% of matcher
  output was re-discovery a full re-match would re-compute."*
- The participation index `_facts_by_rel_slot_val` (`store.py:189`) is, per the
  kernel architecture doc, *"exactly a RETE alpha-memory"*, and the delta +
  `run_seeded` is *"TREAT-like"* semi-naive evaluation
  (`docs/kernel/inference/architecture_and_algorithms.md` O1/O2). ~3.6× over naive.

So E8's relation-keyed "watched-fact" effect is **already shipped** — via
relation-keyed alpha-memory indexing, not per-rule 2-watched-literals. The two
deltas a literal watched-literal scheme would still add are marginal or
deliberately excluded: (a) finer-than-per-relation granularity (a plan with an
unbound entry slot still scans the relation extent, `match.py:133`); (b)
absent-premise plans full-match **by design** — they cannot be seeded without
reintroducing the D2 completeness bug (`saturator.py:77-88`). And the engine's
own CS analysis judges literal watched-literals **premature**: clash detection
is ~0 s (O5), and watched-literals belong to a DPLL/CDCL re-arch *"deferred
because search is not the bottleneck (saturation is)"* (O8, §7).

## Context

DPLL "watched literals" idiom adapted to ein's rules. For each
rule's `:match` clause, pick 2 watch literals; a rule needs
re-evaluation only when one of its watches changes. Avoids
the current "re-iterate every applicable rule on every
saturation pass" cost.

## Tasks

### Task T1.9.E8.1 — Watch-literal selection

For each rule, pick 2 literals from `:match` to watch.
Heuristic: pick literals whose relations are *least frequent*
in the KB (rare relations change less often → fewer wakeups).

### Task T1.9.E8.2 — Watcher index

`(relation, args) → list[rule]` map of watch literals to rules
that watch them. Updated on fact insertion.

### Task T1.9.E8.3 — Saturator integration

When a fact arrives:

- **a.** Look up watchers.
- **b.** Enqueue only watched rules for re-eval.
- **c.** If a watch literal "moves" (the rule's other watch
  fires first), re-pick its watch.

### Task T1.9.E8.4 — Tests + bench

Compare saturation perf with and without watches.

## Acceptance

- Watch index works; saturator wakes correctly on watched-fact
  arrivals.
- Bench shows ≥ 30% saturation speedup on dense rule sets.
