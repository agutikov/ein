## Re-aim (P1.5b): `d` → `(layer, set-size)`

The walkthrough annotates each move with a **branch depth `d`** (the
old ordered tree: `d=0` unconditional, `d=1` inside a hypothesis). The
engine produces a **commitment lattice** with no ordered depth: `d`
maps to **commitment-set size / layer**, and a `1→0 learn no-good` row
maps to a `DeadCommitment.learned_clause`. The diff matches on *named
firing + position-in-layer*, accepting "the same order **or a
recognisably equivalent one**" (idea-08 §acceptance).

## Structural-equivalence notes (per Q-S1.6.5.A)

The matching is structural — the engine may reach the same fact by a
*different but sound* rule than the human named:

- **`range-elimination` ≡ `domain-elimination`.** The human forces a
  *house's* value by excluding the other values
  (`domain-elimination`); the engine, on the gaps path, often forces a
  *value's* house by injective+surjective exclusion
  (`range-elimination`). Both close the same cell. The relevant slice of
  the gaps-primary solution is `range-elimination`-heavy; the full trace
  fires both.
- **`functional` / `total`** are bijection *property* rules. The human
  "by functionality, X is not at `H_1`" surfaces as the
  `functional-negative` firing; "by totality, some value fills `H_1`"
  surfaces as the elimination it enables (`domain-elimination` /
  `range-elimination`). So the named properties are covered by their
  *consequences*, not a standalone firing.
- **fwd/bwd direction** (`adjacent-via-fwd` vs `-bwd`) is a bookkeeping
  split of one non-directional constraint; either counts as the move.

## Granularity (Q-S1.6.5.A working answer)

A human move that the engine reaches by a *chain* of firings (e.g.
"yellow at `H_1`" = three `disjunctive-prune`/`functional-negative`
exclusions then one `domain-elimination`) counts as **one** match —
any trace fragment covering the human prose counts. The `--relevant`
prune (S1.6.5, `ein.trace.relevance`) collapses the 560-firing log
to the ~11 load-bearing firings via a provenance backtrack from the
solved grid, which is the human-scale view this checklist reads.
