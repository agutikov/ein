## Context

Per [P1.8 README Theme A § Standard library](README.md#standard-library): A7
left `std.algebra` with `converse` and the `imply`/`includes` inclusions; A12 is
"the full relation-algebra signature (`compose` / `meet` / `join`)."

A **relation algebra** (Tarski) is ⟨R, ∪, ∩, ¬, 0, 1, ;, ⁻¹, 1'⟩ — a Boolean
algebra over relations-as-pair-sets, plus a monoid under composition `;` with
converse `⁻¹` and identity `1'`. Every rule below is generic, parametrised over
the operand relations (and, for the extensive ops, the universe), activated by a
per-instance fact — exactly the A7 shape.

**The organizing split — intrinsic vs extensive.** This is the design spine:

- **Intrinsic** ops (`compose`, `meet`, `difference`, `converse`, `join`,
  `difunctional`) read only *existing* edges. Pure joins/NAF over the KB; no
  universe; trivially fit the matcher.
- **Extensive** ops (`complement`, `top`, `identity`, `connex`, the residuals)
  need the **universe** — the `Dom×Ran` extent — to range over absent pairs.
  That extent is exactly the `?isa Dom Ran` machinery the
  [A10](s1.8.a10_type_domain_matching.md) typing layer already carries. **So
  A12's Boolean/extensive layer sits on `std.typing` / `std.elim`** — the same
  `?isa` parameter, threaded through. Closed-world, so `complement` inherits the
  [`std.closure`](s1.8.a6_closure_auto_inference.md) soundness caveat (sound only
  when the operand is saturation-determined).

**The other half — the equational theory.** A relation algebra is these
operations *plus* an equational axiomatisation (Tarski / Maddux B1–B10). The
*structural* axioms (B1–B5: Boolean + monoid laws, `A;I = A`, …) hold in ein **by
construction** — ein relations are sets of pairs, i.e. a *representable* RA (RRA),
which is the licence to ship the rest as *sound* lemmas. The *derivational* laws
(B6–B10: involution, contravariance, converse/composition over ∪, and the
**Schröder rule**) interrelate the operators and *do* derive new facts — see
[§ Equational theory](#equational-theory-axioms-as-lemma-rules).

**Expressivity ceiling — RA ≡ FOL³.** Relation algebra captures exactly the
first-order formulas with **≤ 3 variables**. This bounds the library: most zebra
constraints are ≤3-variable and so *are* RA-expressible, but `co-located` (4
relation params) and `adjacent-via` (5) genuinely exceed three logical variables —
they are **not** reducible to relation algebra and correctly stay as named
multi-param rules. The L3 ceiling is the line between "promote into `std.algebra`"
and "keep as a puzzle rule" (the [A5-tail](s1.8.a5_migration_imports.md) question).

## Catalogue

Legend: **⊕** intrinsic/pure · **⊙** needs the `?isa Dom Ran` universe · **⚠**
advanced (forall / generative) · **✅** already shipped (A7 / A10 / zebra2).

### Relative (composition) layer — the keystone

| op | RA | shape | status |
|----|-----|-------|--------|
| **compose** | R₁;R₂ | `(?R1 ?a ?b) ∧ (?R2 ?b ?c) ⟹ (?R3 ?a ?c)` | new ⊕ |
| **converse** | R⁻¹ | `(?R1 ?a ?b) ⟹ (?R2 ?b ?a)` | ✅ A7 |
| **identity** | 1' | `(?isa ?a ?Dom) ⟹ (?R ?a ?a)` | new ⊙ |
| **rel-sum / dagger** | R†S = ¬(¬R;¬S) | derive from complement∘compose | new ⚠ |

```lisp
(rule compose (?R1 ?R2 ?R3)
  :match  (and (?R1 ?a ?b) (?R2 ?b ?c))     ; ?b shared → a Join step
  :assert (?R3 ?a ?c)
  :priority 100
  :why    "(compose {?R1} {?R2} {?R3}): {?R1}({?a},{?b})∧{?R2}({?b},{?c}) → {?R3}({?a},{?c}).")
```

### Boolean (lattice) layer

| op | RA | shape | status |
|----|-----|-------|--------|
| **meet** | R₁∩R₂ | `(?R1 ?a ?b) ∧ (?R2 ?a ?b) ⟹ (?R3 ?a ?b)` | new ⊕ |
| **join** | R₁∪R₂ | `derive-join ()` → `join-l` / `join-r` | new ⊕ |
| **difference** | R₁∖R₂ | `(?R1 ?a ?b) ∧ absent(?R2 ?a ?b) ⟹ (?R3 ?a ?b)` | new ⊕ |
| **complement** | ¬R | `(?isa ?a ?Dom) ∧ (?isa ?b ?Ran) ∧ absent(?R1 ?a ?b) ⟹ (?R2 ?a ?b)` | new ⊙ |
| **top** | 1 | `(?isa ?a ?Dom) ∧ (?isa ?b ?Ran) ⟹ (?R ?a ?b)` | new ⊙ |
| **bottom** | 0 | `(?R ?a ?b) ⟹ (false)` — a *check* (`empty`) | new ⊕ |

**`join` is the first real generic-`or` case**, and it confirms the latent
or-split gap (dual of A11): a generic `(rule join (?R1 ?R2 ?R3) :match (or …))`
would split into `join__or0` / `join__or1`, which the `(join …)` activator can
**never reach** (`store.py:430` registers activators by exact rule name). So
`join` uses the **non-generic fan-out** — the `reflexive` / `bijective-properties`
idiom:

```lisp
(rule derive-join ()                          ; non-generic ⇒ may multi-assert (A11)
  :match  (join ?R1 ?R2 ?R3)
  :assert (and (join-l ?R1 ?R2 ?R3) (join-r ?R1 ?R2 ?R3)))
(rule join-l (?R1 ?R2 ?R3) :match (?R1 ?a ?b) :assert (?R3 ?a ?b) :priority 100)
(rule join-r (?R1 ?R2 ?R3) :match (?R2 ?a ?b) :assert (?R3 ?a ?b) :priority 100)
```

```lisp
;; complement — the only op that materialises absent pairs; needs the universe.
(rule complement (?R1 ?R2 ?isa ?Dom ?Ran)
  :match  (and (?isa ?a ?Dom) (?isa ?b ?Ran) (absent (?R1 ?a ?b)))
  :assert (?R2 ?a ?b)
  :priority 100
  :why    "(complement {?R1} {?R2}): {?a}∈{?Dom},{?b}∈{?Ran},¬{?R1}({?a},{?b}) → {?R2}({?a},{?b}).")
```

### Peircean derived ops + residuals

Two senses of "residual" — keep them distinct (the [Wikipedia RA article](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Relation_algebra)
has only the first):

- **Peircean derived ops** (in the RA signature) — `A ◃ B = A;B˘` and
  `A ▹ B = A˘;B`. Just composition∘converse, so **intrinsic ⊕** — derivable by
  chaining `compose` + `converse`, no new machinery. Ship as ergonomic aliases
  (or leave as a documented `compose`-then-`converse` idiom).
- **Division residuals** (allegory / Heyting extension, *beyond* Tarski's RA, not
  in the article) — `R\S` = largest X with `R;X ⊆ S`; dually `R/S`; and
  `syq(R,S) = (R\S) ∩ (R°/S°)`. These need a universally-quantified `forall` over
  the universe (**⚠**), have no zebra consumer, and are deferred. Sketch:

```lisp
(rule left-residual (?R ?S ?X ?isa ?Dom ?Cod)
  :match  (and (?isa ?b ?Dom) (?isa ?c ?Cod)
               (forall ?a (?R ?a ?b) (?S ?a ?c)))
  :assert (?X ?b ?c) :priority 90)
```

### Equational theory: axioms as lemma rules

A relation algebra is the operations *plus* the Tarski/Maddux axioms B1–B10.
B1–B5 (Boolean + monoid structure) hold by construction (RRA); B6–B10 are
*derivational* — lemmas that interrelate the operator facts, in the spirit of
A7's `symmetric ⟺ converse R R`, all reflective (A9: a derived operator fact
lights up its rule next pass).

| law | equation | lemma (over operator facts) | status |
|-----|----------|------------------------------|--------|
| **B6** involution | `A˘˘ = A` | ≈ `converse-pair-symmetric` | ✅ A7 |
| **B7** contravariance | `(A;B)˘ = B˘;A˘` | `(compose R S T)` + converses ⟹ `(compose S° R° T°)` | new ⊕ |
| **B8** converse/∪ | `(A∨B)˘ = A˘∨B˘` | `(join R S U)` ⟹ `(join R° S° U°)` | new ⊕ |
| **B9** comp/∪ distrib | `(A∨B);C = A;C ∨ B;C` | relate `join` + `compose` facts | new ⊕ |
| **B10** **Schröder** | `A;B ≤ C⁻ ⟺ A˘;C ≤ B⁻ ⟺ C;B˘ ≤ A⁻` | negative propagation (below) | new ⚠ |

**B10 (Schröder / cycle law) is the high-value one** — operationally, for a
declared composite `T = R;S`, a *missing composite edge forces a missing factor
edge*: `T(a,c) = ∃b. R(a,b)∧S(b,c)`, so `¬T(a,c) ∧ R(a,b) ⟹ ¬S(b,c)`.

```lisp
(rule compose-negative-s (?R ?S ?T)
  :match  (and (?R ?a ?b) (not (?T ?a ?c)))
  :assert (not (?S ?b ?c)) :priority 240)
(rule compose-negative-r (?R ?S ?T)
  :match  (and (?S ?b ?c) (not (?T ?a ?c)))
  :assert (not (?R ?a ?b)) :priority 240)
```

This is the **generic form of zebra2's `adjacent-via` / `disjunctive-prune`**
negative propagation (those *are* compositions through a spatial relation), so
it's a candidate to subsume those hand-written rules (Q-S1.8.A12.E). Sound only
when `T = R;S` is *closed* — composition is the only source of `T` (the
[`std.closure`](s1.8.a6_closure_auto_inference.md) caveat).

### Single-relation properties as RA equations (bridge to existing tags)

The tags zebra2 uses *are* RA equations; listing them shows the gaps:

| property | RA equation | status |
|----------|-------------|--------|
| reflexive / irreflexive | 1'⊆R / R∩1'=0 | ✅ A10 / new ⊕ (`(?R ?a ?a)⟹⊥`) |
| symmetric / antisymmetric / asymmetric | R=R° / R∩R°⊆1' / R∩R°=0 | ✅ A7 / new ⊕ / new ⊕ |
| transitive / dense | R;R⊆R / R⊆R;R | ✅ zebra2 / new ⚠ (generative) |
| functional / injective | R°;R⊆1' / R;R°⊆1' | ✅ / ✅ |
| total / surjective / bijective | 1'⊆R;R° / 1'⊆R°;R / all four | ✅ / ✅ / ✅ |
| connex | R∪R°=1 | new ⊙ |
| difunctional | R;R°;R⊆R | new ⊕ |
| equivalence | reflexive∧symmetric∧transitive | composable |

### Task T1.8.A12.4 — Equational lemmas (Schröder / contravariance / distributivity)

The B6–B10 derivational laws (§ Equational theory). Highest-value:

- **a. Schröder / cycle (B10)** — `compose-negative-{r,s}`: a stored `(not (T a c))`
  on a declared composite `T = R;S` propagates to `(not (S b c))` / `(not (R a b))`.
  The generic form of zebra2's `adjacent-via` / `disjunctive-prune`. Gate on
  `T = R;S` *closed* (the `std.closure` caveat).
- **b. Contravariance (B7)** — `(compose R S T)` + converses ⟹ `(compose S° R° T°)`.
- **c. Converse-/composition-over-join (B8/B9)** — relate `join` + `converse` /
  `compose` operator facts (reflective, A9).

### Task T1.8.A12.5 — Residuals (deferred, allegory completion)

are intrinsic and fold into T1.8.A12.1). forall over the universe; no M1
consumer. Ship the design here; implement on a concrete need.

## Open questions

- **Q-S1.8.A12.A — One module or split?** Keep the algebra in `std.algebra`, or
  hive the universe-dependent ops into `std.typing` (where the extent lives)?
  Leaning: operators in `std.algebra`, the `(type-hierarchy …)` extent knob in
  `std.typing`, mirroring the A10 converse split (b in algebra, c in typing).
- **Q-S1.8.A12.B — `compose` arity / chains.** Only binary `;`; an n-ary
  `(compose-chain R1 … Rn Out)` would need a variadic slot the matcher lacks.
  Document the "compose twice" idiom instead?
- **Q-S1.8.A12.C — NAF stratification.** `difference` / `complement` are NAF over
  another relation; `absents_still_pass` closes the enqueue-time race, but a
  *priority* ordering (operand saturates before the op fires) keeps traces sane.
  Pin the band.
- **Q-S1.8.A12.D — `dense` / generative properties.** `dense` (R⊆R;R) needs an
  *invented* midpoint — not a check. Skip, or express as a hypgen generator?
- **Q-S1.8.A12.E — Subsume the hand-written negative rules?** Once expressed as
  compositions, do `compose-negative-{r,s}` (Schröder) + the converse lemmas
  subsume zebra2's `adjacent-via` / `disjunctive-prune` / `co-located-negative`?
  Migration question, gated on the closed-composite caveat and trace legibility
  (the named rules read better in a human trace). Feeds the A5-tail.
- **Q-S1.8.A12.F — Q-pairing for n-ary?** The article's Q0–Q2 pairing axioms
  (`A˘;A ≤ I`, `B˘;B ≤ I`, `A˘;B = 1`) encode ordered pairs with projections —
  RA's route to >2-ary relations, hence the >3-variable constraints the L3
  ceiling excludes (`co-located` / `adjacent-via`). Pursue as the algebraic basis
  for higher arity, or keep the multi-param named rules?
  ([F1b](../../followups/f1b_logical_formulation.md) territory.)
